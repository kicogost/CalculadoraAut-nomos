## Themes (ranked)
1. **"Get a gestor, don't DIY — it's too complicated"** — overwhelming majority. Even an
   accountant outsources it.
2. **Set-aside behaviour is real** — separate account "so I'm not tempted", "set aside
   30% for when the fine comes". Manual, anxiety-driven.
3. **Fear of fines / fatalism / distrust** — "you'll end up paying Hacienda."
4. **Gestor quality is hit-or-miss** — "4th accountant… many incompetent ones."
5. **English-speaking gestor gap** (expat-specific) — repeated demand; a directory
   (YourMatePat) already exists for it.
6. **Minority: DIY is fine for simple freelancers** (Puzzleheaded-Sun7418).

## What it means for Provisio

**Validates**
- The **"cuánto apartar / beneficio neto"** hook — people literally run manual
  set-aside accounts and 30% rules. This is a felt, unmet need *even for gestor users*
  (the gestor doesn't tell them in real time). Lead with it.
- **Gestor trust/quality is shaky** (4 accountants, "incompetent ones") → an opening.

**Challenges (take seriously)**
- Strong resistance to **DIY / "replace your gestor"** — "no way in hell", "even I (an
  accountant) pay someone". The pure self-serve message hits a **trust + complexity
  wall**. Our edge has to be *confidence/correctness*, not just "do it yourself".
- Supports leaning on **"we pre-fill everything, you just approve"** rather than "you do
  your taxes" — but even that must overcome the "I'd rather a human did it" instinct.

**Caveats**
- **Wrong ICP for this thread** (expats, language barrier) → they over-index on "get a
  gestor". Don't over-update. The 20–35 software-native *Spanish* autónomo may feel very
  differently — **probe this exact trust barrier in the real interviews.**
- Comments are short; this is signal, not depth.
- **Competitor/adjacent noted:** YourMatePat (English-speaking gestor directory); Holded
  confirmed in active use (matches `docs/COMPETITORS.md`).

**→ Interview probe to add:** *"Would you ever trust an app (vs a person) with your
taxes? What exactly would it have to do for you to feel safe?"* The trust barrier is the
crux this thread surfaced.
